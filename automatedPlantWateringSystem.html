// 01 — Introduction

Overview

This is my final project for Introduction to Mechanical Engineering: Programming and Hardware Interface (MEGN200). It was a formative experience that sparked my interest in embedded systems and hardware-software integration.

The project gave me hands-on practice connecting sensors, actuators, and microcontrollers — and valuable experience working in a small team: collaborating, dividing work, and communicating through the design and build process.

// 04 — Sensors

Sensor Functionality

Three sensor/output components work in concert to automate the watering decision and communicate system state to the user.

Moisture Sensor

Detects changes in soil capacitance caused by varying moisture levels. Outputs volumetric water content — a ratio of water volume to total volume — ranging from 1 (submerged) to 667 (bone dry).

Temp / Humidity

A capacitive humidity sensor and thermistor combo. The capacitive element tracks water vapor in air; the thermistor converts resistance to temperature. Outputs relative humidity (%) and temperature.

LCD Display

A simple LCD module that cycles through temperature, humidity, and soil volumetric water content in 5-second intervals — giving the user a live view of all monitored conditions.
