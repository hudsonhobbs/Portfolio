System Hardware

Above, on the left, is the motor that moves the water from the tank at the bottom of the pot to the plant at the top. The middle image shows the sensors that determine when the plant should be watered. The two-pronged sensor monitors soil moisture, and the second sensor monitors ambient temperature and humidity. Lastly, in the image on the left, a digital display shows the outputs of those sensors.

Sensor Functionality

Moisture Sensor: Uses a circuit to detect changes in capacitance caused by varying moisture levels. By measuring soil charge, the sensor calculates moisture level and outputs volumetric water content, a ratio of water volume to total volume. Ranging from 1 (completely submerged) to 667 (completely dry)

Temperature/Humidity Sensor: Uses a capacitive humidity sensor that absorbs water vapor, changing the dielectric constant of the sensor; the effective capacitance changes, recalculating relative humidity as a percentage. It also uses a thermistor, a temperature-sensitive resistor, to calculate and output temperature from its resistance.

Digital Display: Is a simple LCD Display, which cycles through temperature, humidity, and soil volumetric water content in 5-second intervals.
