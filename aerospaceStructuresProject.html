// 01 — Overview

Project Overview

This project focused on designing and evaluating a structural beam cross section for a communication satellite under specified loading conditions. The objective was to find the lowest-cost cross-section that meets structural demands — minimizing mass while maintaining required factors of safety.

I used SkyCiv to perform the primary structural analysis, then independently validated results using SolidWorks Simulation to cross-check stress and deflection outputs.

Fig. 1 — Isometric view of beam model in SolidWorks.
// 02 — Requirements

Requirements & Constraints

  • Material: 6061-T6 Aluminum Alloy
  • Web / Flange geometry: Dimensions must not exceed 700 mm; wall thickness ≥ 7 mm
  • Objective: Minimize cost while satisfying stress, deflection, and FoS requirements under the given loading
Fig. 2 — Problem setup: free body diagram with loads and boundary conditions.
// 03 — Approach

Candidate Cross Sections

I evaluated I, T, and C-channel cross-section geometries using the thin-walled beam assumption. For each candidate, I compared second moment of area, torsional rigidity, shear flow, and total beam mass to identify the most structurally efficient option.
