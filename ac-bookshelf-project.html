Project 02 — CAD & FEA

AC Bookshelf

Problem-solving through SolidWorks CAD, additive manufacturing, and finite element analysis.

SolidWorks FEA / Simulation 3D Printing PLA BambuLab X1 Summer 2025
// 01 — Problem Statement

The Problem

I needed a place to store books above my air conditioning unit — without blocking airflow, creating structural risk, or wasting the dead space where a wooden brace sat. The structure needed to be compact, sturdy, and capable of handling uneven load distribution.

This project also served as a practical application of FEA concepts from a course I took in Summer 2025 — turning classroom theory into real-world structural validation.

Fig. 1 — Initial space constraint showing AC unit and available clearance.
// 02 — Design & Fabrication

Proposed Solution

After measuring available space and clearance, I designed a custom 3D-printed bracket and shelf system in SolidWorks. The design mounts around the AC unit, preserving airflow while filling dead space.

When sliced in Bambu Studio, I found the original part exceeded the print volume — so I split it into two interlocking halves with complementary studs, holes, and toleranced mating surfaces. The final parts were printed on a BambuLab X1 at the Mines makerspace.

Fig. 2 — SolidWorks CAD model with split-part strategy.
// 03 — Final Result

Final Implementation

After a few print iterations, the final product was installed and is currently holding over 12 lbs of books and gear — with no measurable deflection or concern for structural failure.

Fig. 3 — Installed shelf loaded with ~12 lbs of books.
// 04 — Structural Validation

FEA Analysis

To validate structural integrity, I performed a finite element analysis using SolidWorks Simulation. The model was constrained at mounting points and loaded at the simulated maximum of 40 lbs — double the intended load to account for the non-solid diamond infill in the 3D-printed parts.
